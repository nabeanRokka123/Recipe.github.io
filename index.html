Gundruk Jhol

Gundruk jhol is a hearty soup made from fermented lentil leaves. It is a popular dish in the Himalayan region and is said to have medicinal properties. The leaves are fermented for several weeks, which gives the soup a unique flavor and texture. Gundruk jhol is typically served with rice or roti.

Daal Bhaat

Dal bhat is the national dish of Nepal and is a staple food for many people. It consists of rice, lentil soup, and a vegetable curry. The lentil soup is made with red lentils, onions, tomatoes, and spices. The vegetable curry can be made with a variety of vegetables, such as potatoes, tomatoes, cauliflower, and spinach.

Pizza

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.).

Thupka

Thukpa is a comforting noodle soup made with meat or vegetables and is a staple food in Nepal and Tibet. It is a hearty and flavorful dish that is perfect for a cold day. Thukpa can be made with a variety of different noodles, including wheat noodles, rice noodles, or buckwheat noodles.

MO:MO

Momos are a type of steamed dumpling that is popular in Nepal, Tibet, and other parts of Asia. They are typically filled with ground meat, vegetables, or cheese. Momos can be served steamed, fried, or boiled. They are often served with a dipping sauce made from tomato chutney or chili sauce.

Laphing

Laphing is a cold, spicy noodle salad from Nepal. It is made with buckwheat noodles, vegetables, and spices. The noodles are cooked and then chilled, then tossed with a dressing made from soy sauce, vinegar, chili oil, and spices. The vegetables are typically julienned or shredded, and may include carrots, cucumbers, onions, and tomatoes. Laphing is often served as a snack or light meal, and can be garnished with fresh cilantro or mint.
